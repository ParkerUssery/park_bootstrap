Travis Strikes Again: No More Heroes

The story follows Travis Touchdown, an ex-assassin who escaped his past life to live in the forest and play video-games.

Unbeknownst to him, he is the target of Badman, a man out for revenge after the death of his daughter, Bad Girl after her duel with Travis.

But they're soon forced to work together after it's discovered that Travis is in posession of a DeathDrive Mark-II. A cursed game console that is fabled to grant the wish of those who beat all 6 games, but kills all who fail.

And so begins a wild, surprisingly hearfelt journey to obtain all six legendary Death Balls, and bring back Badman's daughter.

WTF: Work Time Fun

The best way to describe this peculiar game is by its Japanese title, Part-Time Hell. This "game" is not so much a game, as it is a collection of various mini-games all themed after soul-crushing part-time jobs.

On the surface, this may seem like something akin to Warioware, but play it and you'll find a surreal, comedic, bizzarely entertaining exercize in tedium.

The Missing: J.J. Macfield and the Island of Memories

Off the coast of Maine exists the titular Island of Memories, where the titular J.J. Macfield and her girlfriend Emily decide to go camping. However, not all is right on this mysterious enclave.

After a brutal storm, J.J. awakes to find Emily missing, and a newfound inability to die. She now must brave the horrors of this dreamlike island, and literally get torn limb-from-limb to get her girlfriend back.

Pathologic (1 & 2)

In a vauge point in time, in the mystical steppe settlesment of Town-on-Gorkhon, a plague has broken out. You're starved, thirsty, exhausted, sick, framed for murder, and you're the town's only hope of stopping this disease.

In Pathologic, you're only given seven days to put a stop to the plague before it spreads to the rest of the world, all while navigating the confusing social structure and doing your best to gain the trust of the people. Nothing in this survival-rpg horror / misery simulator is going to be simple. You have to constantly be on your toes to avoid the seemingly infinite dangers this dying town hides.
